# Competitor Research — Example Output

Use this as your quality bar. Match the depth, specificity, and formatting exactly.

---

## Example: Product idea = "AI contract review tool for freelancers"

### Ironclad
- **Website:** [ironcladapp.com](https://ironcladapp.com)
- **Pricing:** Starts at $2,000/month (enterprise). No self-serve tier. Minimum 3-seat commitment.
- **Key features:** Automated redlining, clause library, approval workflows, Salesforce/DocuSign integrations, full audit trail.
- **Weaknesses:** Built for legal teams and enterprises, not individual freelancers. Pricing is 40–100x what a solo contractor can justify. Onboarding takes weeks. No per-document pricing option.

### Contractbook
- **Website:** [contractbook.com](https://contractbook.com)
- **Pricing:** Free for 3 contracts/month. Pro at $49/month. Business at $99/month.
- **Key features:** Template library, e-signature, data extraction, Zapier integration, version history.
- **Weaknesses:** Review is template-based, not AI-powered analysis of uploaded arbitrary contracts. Requires both parties to use the platform. Poor at surfacing non-standard risk clauses in client-supplied PDFs.

### DocuSign Insight
- **Website:** [docusign.com/products/insight](https://www.docusign.com/products/insight)
- **Pricing:** Add-on to DocuSign Business Pro ($65/month) and above. Insight is enterprise-only with custom pricing.
- **Key features:** AI clause extraction, risk scoring, search across contract repository, integrates with DocuSign envelope workflow.
- **Weaknesses:** Only useful if you already use DocuSign. No standalone contract review. UI is clunky for quick one-off reviews. Overkill for freelancers doing 5–10 contracts per year.

### LawGeex
- **Website:** [lawgeex.com](https://lawgeex.com)
- **Pricing:** Not public. Demo required. Enterprise-only based on volume.
- **Key features:** AI compares your contract against pre-set policy playbooks, flags deviations, returns redlined version. Used by in-house legal teams.
- **Weaknesses:** Requires building a policy playbook before it can review anything — a weeks-long setup. No useful for ad-hoc freelancer review. Zero self-serve.

### **Gap this product fills**
Existing tools are built for legal departments with budgets and IT support. No tool on the market offers instant, pay-per-review AI analysis of an arbitrary client-supplied contract for a solo freelancer. The sweet spot: under $10/review, no account required, results in under 60 seconds.

---

## Formatting Rules

1. Every competitor MUST have all four fields: Website (with working https:// link), Pricing (specific numbers, not "varies"), Key features (3–5 concrete capabilities), Weaknesses (specific to WHY this competitor fails the target user — not generic).
2. Close with a **Gap** paragraph that synthesises what the competitors don't do — this becomes the product's wedge.
3. Do not invent pricing or features. If pricing is not public, say "Not public — demo required."
4. Use specific numbers wherever available: seat counts, price tiers, usage limits.
